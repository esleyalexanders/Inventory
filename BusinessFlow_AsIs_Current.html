4. Current Pain Points

❌ Manual Data Entry Across Spreadsheets

Problem: Sales enters order in one spreadsheet, planning uses another, warehouse maintains separate stock lists. Same data typed multiple times with high risk of errors and inconsistencies.

Impact: Data errors, version control issues, time wasted on data entry and reconciliation.

❌ No Real-Time Inventory Visibility

Problem: Stock levels are tracked in spreadsheets updated manually. To check stock, must email or call warehouse staff. No way to see real-time availability or what's reserved for other orders.

Impact: Double-booking stock, inaccurate delivery promises, conflicts between orders, delayed responses to customers.

❌ Lead Time Uncertainty from Outsourcing Vendors

Problem: Vendor lead times are confirmed via email/phone with no system tracking. Delays not communicated proactively. No visibility into vendor production progress.

Impact: Delivery date commitments at risk, last-minute firefighting, customer dissatisfaction, inability to plan around vendor delays.

❌ Capacity Planning Guesswork

Problem: Production capacity estimated manually based on gut feel and rough calculations. No systematic view of current workload, machine availability, or operator capacity.

Impact: Overcommitting or underutilizing capacity, unrealistic production schedules, frequent rescheduling and chaos.

❌ Informal Reservations Cause Conflicts

Problem: Stock reservations done via email notes or verbal communication. Multiple orders may claim the same stock. No formal allocation system.

Impact: Conflicts when picking for shipment, orders delayed because stock allocated elsewhere, finger-pointing between sales and warehouse.

❌ Scattered Communications (Email/Chat/Phone)

Problem: Order status, stock inquiries, delivery updates, quality issues all communicated via different channels. No centralized record of decisions or issues.

Impact: Information lost, decisions forgotten, new staff can't find historical context, constant interruptions asking for status updates.

❌ Delayed Quality Feedback Loops

Problem: Quality inspections recorded on paper forms. Defects reported verbally or via chat. Vendor quality issues communicated slowly via email.

Impact: Defective materials used in production before QC results available, vendor issues not escalated quickly, rework costs high, compliance documentation incomplete.

❌ Management Lacks Consolidated Dashboard

Problem: To understand business status, management must request ad-hoc reports from each department. Data compiled manually in spreadsheets. Reporting takes days and data is already outdated.

Impact: Reactive management instead of proactive, decisions based on old data, inability to spot trends or issues early, no KPI visibility.

5. Process Inefficiencies by Department

Sales Department

  • Manual PO entry into spreadsheets with risk of typos
  • Manually verify BOM codes against master lists
  • No instant visibility into stock availability or production capacity
  • Cannot give customers immediate delivery date commitments
  • Constant back-and-forth with planning for order feasibility

Planning Department

  • Manual BOM explosion in Excel for every order
  • Must request stock counts from warehouse (no real-time view)
  • Capacity planning based on estimates and memory, not system data
  • In-house vs outsource decisions made without full cost/lead time comparison
  • Material available dates calculated manually with high risk of errors
  • No automated alerts when materials will be late

Procurement Department

  • Shortage lists collected manually from planning spreadsheets
  • RFQs and POs created manually and sent via email
  • Vendor lead time confirmations tracked in spreadsheets or email folders
  • Must manually chase vendors for order status updates
  • No systematic vendor performance tracking

Warehouse Department

  • Stock counts maintained in separate spreadsheets (not integrated)
  • Informal reservation system via email creates conflicts
  • Paper pick lists for material issue to production
  • Manual inventory updates after receipts and issues
  • No automated low stock alerts
  • Shipment coordination via phone and email

Production Department

  • Job schedules written on whiteboard or paper
  • Progress reported via chat messages or photos
  • No systematic work order management
  • Material shortages discovered when production starts (too late)
  • Rework and scrap logged manually on paper forms

Quality Control Department

  • Inspection results recorded on paper forms
  • Defect notifications via verbal communication or chat
  • No systematic tracking of reject rates or trends
  • Vendor quality issues communicated slowly via email
  • Compliance documentation scattered across paper files

Finance Department

  • Invoice creation manual after receiving shipment notification
  • Payment tracking in separate accounting system
  • No integration with sales orders or delivery status
  • Receivables aging reports created manually

Management

  • Must request status updates from each department
  • No real-time dashboard showing overall business health
  • Ad-hoc reports compiled manually and already outdated
  • Cannot quickly analyze trends or identify bottlenecks
  • Reactive firefighting instead of proactive management

💡 How the Automated To-Be Solution Addresses These Issues

Every pain point identified in the current As-Is process is eliminated in the automated ERP system:

  • Single data entry point - Order entered once, flows through entire system automatically
  • Real-time inventory visibility - All users see current stock levels and reservations instantly
  • Vendor integration - Digital portals provide real-time lead time and delivery tracking
  • Automated capacity planning - System calculates feasibility and suggests optimal paths
  • Formal stock reservations - System manages allocations, eliminates conflicts
  • Centralized communication - All order information, notes, and decisions in one system
  • Instant quality feedback - QC results update stock status immediately, vendors notified automatically
  • Real-time dashboards - Management has instant visibility into all KPIs and exceptions
