Business Flow: Shipping & Invoicing

Automated pick/pack operations, shipping documentation, invoice generation, and delivery tracking

2. Flow Chart Explanation

This shipping and invoicing workflow automates the entire order fulfillment process from pick/pack to delivery and payment collection. The system generates picking lists automatically, manages shipping documentation, creates invoices upon shipment, and tracks delivery and payment status. Exception-based workflow means warehouse and accounting teams only intervene for variances or payment issues.

Process Flow Steps

Step 1: Order Ready to Ship

Trigger: Order approved and goods available for shipment.

Prerequisites met:

  • Customer order validated and confirmed
  • Finished goods available in inventory (stock status: Available)
  • Customer credit limit approved (if applicable)
  • Requested delivery date approaching or ready for immediate ship
  • No holds or blocks on customer account

System: Order status is "Ready for Picking"

Step 2: System Generates Pick List

Action: System automatically creates warehouse pick list.

Pick list contains:

  • Sales order number and customer name
  • All items to be shipped with quantities
  • Storage locations in warehouse (bin/shelf/rack)
  • Batch/lot numbers if applicable
  • Picking sequence (optimized for warehouse layout)
  • Special handling instructions

Automation: Pick list generated automatically - no manual preparation required

Optimization: System groups multiple orders for efficient batch picking if enabled

Step 3: Warehouse Picks Goods

Action: Warehouse staff picks items per pick list.

Picking activities:

  • Navigate to storage locations following pick sequence
  • Retrieve items from shelves/bins
  • Verify item code and description
  • Count quantity to be picked
  • Stage picked goods in packing area

Tools: Mobile scanner, pick list printout, or pick-by-light system

Step 4: Scan & Confirm Picked Quantity

Action: Picker scans items and confirms actual picked quantity.

Confirmation process:

  • Scan item barcode to verify correct product
  • Enter or confirm picked quantity
  • System validates against ordered quantity
  • Timestamp and picker ID recorded

System: Compares picked vs ordered quantity

Step 5: Picked Quantity Match Decision

Decision: Does picked quantity match ordered quantity?

If Yes (Exact Match): Proceed to packing (normal flow)

If No (Shortage or Overage): Flag variance for review

Common variance reasons:

  • Insufficient stock available (inventory error)
  • Damaged goods discovered during picking
  • Wrong item in storage location
  • Picker error (wrong quantity counted)

Step 6: Flag Shortage/Variance

Action: System flags the quantity discrepancy.

System records:

  • Ordered quantity vs picked quantity
  • Shortage amount
  • Item(s) affected
  • Picker notes/reason for variance

Step 7: Notify Sales Team

Action: System automatically alerts sales team of shortage.

Notification contains:

  • Customer name and order number
  • Items short and quantity variance
  • Reason for shortage
  • Options for resolution

Delivery method: Email, dashboard alert, or mobile notification

Step 8: Manual Review & Decision

Action: Sales team reviews shortage and determines action.

Review considerations:

  • Customer priority and relationship
  • Impact of partial shipment
  • Availability of shortage items (when can we get them)
  • Customer preference (wait for complete order or accept partial)

Step 9: Partial Ship Decision

Decision: Should we ship partial order or wait for complete availability?

If Yes (Partial Ship):

  • Adjust order quantity to picked quantity
  • Create backorder for shortage items
  • Notify customer of partial shipment
  • Proceed to packing

If No (Wait for Complete):

  • Cancel or postpone shipment
  • Return picked goods to inventory
  • Wait for shortage items to become available

Step 10: Cancel/Postpone Shipment

Action: Shipment is held until shortage resolved.

System actions:

  • Release stock reservation (return to available inventory)
  • Order status updated to "On Hold - Awaiting Stock"
  • Customer notified of delay
  • Expected ship date updated

Step 11: System Generates Packing List

Action: System automatically creates packing list for shipment.

Packing list contains:

  • Customer name and delivery address
  • Sales order number and date
  • Complete list of items being shipped with quantities
  • Item descriptions and part numbers
  • Total weight and dimensions (if applicable)
  • Special handling instructions

Automation: Packing list auto-generated from picked quantities - no manual typing

Step 12: Pack Goods

Action: Warehouse staff packs goods for shipment.

Packing activities:

  • Select appropriate packaging (box, pallet, crate)
  • Pack items securely with protective materials
  • Include packing list in shipment
  • Seal and label package
  • Weigh and measure packed shipment

Step 13: System Generates Shipping Docs

Action: System automatically creates shipping documentation.

Shipping documents include:

  • Delivery note/waybill
  • Commercial invoice (for export shipments)
  • Packing list (customer copy)
  • Certificate of origin (if required)
  • Customs declaration (for international shipments)

Automation: All documents populated from order data - no manual data entry

Step 14: Select Carrier & Print Labels

Action: System assigns carrier and generates shipping labels.

Carrier selection logic:

  • Customer's preferred carrier (if specified)
  • Delivery location and service level
  • Package weight and dimensions
  • Cost optimization rules

Label generation:

  • Carrier-specific label format
  • Tracking number assigned
  • Destination address and barcode
  • Service level (standard, express, overnight)

Integration: Direct carrier API integration for label generation (FedEx, UPS, DHL, etc.)

Step 15: Handover to Carrier

Action: Physical shipment handed over to logistics carrier.

Handover process:

  • Carrier pickup or drop-off at carrier depot
  • Carrier scans and accepts shipment
  • Proof of handover received

Step 16: System Logs Shipment

Action: System records shipment details and tracking info.

Shipment record includes:

  • Shipment date and time
  • Carrier name and service level
  • Tracking number
  • Package count, weight, and dimensions
  • Shipped quantities per item
  • Shipping personnel ID

Step 17: Update Order Status: Shipped

Action: System automatically updates order status.

Status updates:

  • Order status: "Shipped"
  • Ship date recorded
  • Inventory reduced (goods removed from warehouse)
  • Tracking number linked to order

Notifications sent to:

  • Customer: Shipment confirmation with tracking number
  • Sales rep: Order shipped notification
  • Accounting: Trigger for invoice generation

Step 18: System Auto-Generates Invoice

Action: Invoice created automatically upon shipment.

Invoice details auto-populated:

  • Customer billing information from master data
  • Items shipped with quantities and unit prices
  • Subtotal, taxes, shipping charges
  • Payment terms (net 30, net 60, etc.)
  • Invoice date (shipment date)
  • Due date calculated from payment terms
  • Invoice number (auto-generated sequential)

Automation: No manual invoice typing - all data from sales order and shipment

Step 19: Invoice Sent to Customer

Action: Invoice delivered to customer automatically.

Delivery methods:

  • Email: PDF invoice to customer's billing email
  • Customer Portal: Posted to online portal for download
  • EDI: Electronic Data Interchange for large customers
  • Print & Mail: For customers requiring paper invoices

System: Tracks invoice sent date and delivery confirmation

Step 20: System Tracks Delivery

Action: System monitors shipment delivery status via carrier tracking.

Tracking updates:

  • In transit status updates
  • Expected delivery date
  • Delivery exceptions (delays, issues)
  • Delivery confirmation (signed receipt)

Integration: Automatic tracking updates via carrier API

Step 21: Delivery Confirmed Decision

Decision: Has carrier confirmed successful delivery?

Confirmed: Carrier provides proof of delivery (signature, photo, GPS)

Not Confirmed: Shipment still in transit or delivery attempted but failed

Step 22: Update Order Status: Delivered

Action: System updates order to delivered status.

Status updates:

  • Order status: "Delivered"
  • Actual delivery date recorded
  • Proof of delivery (POD) saved in system
  • Delivery recipient name captured

Notifications:

  • Sales team: Order successfully delivered
  • Customer service: For customer inquiries reference

Step 23: Trigger Payment Tracking

Action: System activates payment monitoring workflow.

Payment tracking initiated:

  • Invoice recorded in Accounts Receivable (AR)
  • Payment due date calculated
  • Automated payment reminders scheduled
  • AR aging bucket assigned (current, 30 days, 60 days, 90+ days)

Step 24: Monitor Payment Due Date

Action: System monitors invoice payment status daily.

Monitoring activities:

  • Countdown to payment due date
  • Check for payment receipt
  • Calculate days overdue if past due date

Step 25: Payment Received Decision

Decision: Has customer paid the invoice?

Payment methods tracked:

  • Bank transfer/wire
  • Check deposit
  • Credit card payment
  • Online payment portal

If Yes (Payment Received): Close order and update AR

If No (Not Paid): Send payment reminder

Step 26: Close Order & Update AR

Action: Order closed and payment recorded.

System updates:

  • Invoice status: "Paid"
  • Payment date and amount recorded
  • Payment method noted
  • Accounts Receivable balance reduced
  • Cash/bank account increased
  • Order lifecycle complete

Analytics: Payment captured for cash flow reporting and DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) calculation

Step 27: Send Payment Reminder

Action: System sends automated payment reminder to customer.

Reminder schedule:

  • Friendly reminder: 3-5 days before due date
  • Due date reminder: On due date if not paid
  • Overdue reminder: 7 days, 14 days, 30 days past due

Reminder contains:

  • Invoice number and amount
  • Original due date
  • Days overdue (if applicable)
  • Payment instructions
  • Invoice copy attached

Step 28: Overdue Decision

Decision: Is invoice overdue beyond grace period?

Grace period: Typically 30-60 days depending on customer terms

If Yes (Seriously Overdue): Escalate to collections

If No (Within Grace): Continue monitoring and reminders

Step 29: Escalate to Collections

Action: Overdue invoice escalated for intensive collection efforts.

Collections actions:

  • Personal phone call from AR manager
  • Customer account put on credit hold (no new orders)
  • Dispute resolution if customer claims issues
  • Payment plan negotiation
  • Legal action consideration (for large amounts)
  • Bad debt provision (if uncollectible)

System: Flags customer for credit review and future order approval

Key Automation Points

✓ Automatic Pick List Generation

Pick lists created automatically from sales orders with optimized picking sequence and locations - no manual list preparation.

✓ Automated Shipping Documentation

Packing lists, delivery notes, and shipping labels generated automatically - all documents populated from order data with no manual typing.

✓ Carrier Integration & Tracking

Direct integration with carriers (FedEx, UPS, DHL) for automated label generation, tracking number assignment, and delivery status updates.

✓ Automatic Invoice Generation

Invoices created automatically upon shipment with all details from sales order - no manual invoice typing or calculation errors.

✓ Automated Customer Notifications

Customers receive shipment confirmations, tracking numbers, and invoices automatically via email or portal - no manual communication required.

✓ Real-Time Delivery Tracking

System monitors delivery status automatically via carrier APIs - provides visibility without manual tracking number checks.

✓ Automated Payment Reminders

Payment reminders sent automatically based on due dates and aging - reduces manual follow-up and improves cash collection.

✓ Exception-Based Fulfillment

Orders flow automatically from picking to delivery to payment. Teams only intervene for quantity variances, delivery failures, or payment issues.
